# Sample CTF Reflection

## Challenge Summary

This challenge required me to investigate a simulated cybersecurity problem and determine the correct solution using command-line tools, web testing, and research.

## Objective

The goal was to understand the challenge prompt, identify the correct attack or analysis method, and submit the correct answer in the CTF platform.

## Tools Used

- Kali Linux
- Terminal
- curl
- grep
- Browser
- Notes/documentation

## What I Learned

I learned how important it is to test one small idea at a time instead of guessing randomly. I also became more comfortable using Linux commands to inspect files, send web requests, and review output. This helped me understand how cybersecurity professionals break down problems during investigations.

## Mistakes or Inefficiencies

One mistake I made was spending too much time trying different ideas without documenting each attempt clearly. In the future, I would keep better notes from the beginning so I can track what worked, what failed, and why.

## Future Improvement

For future CTF challenges, I would start by reading the briefing carefully, writing down the objective, and identifying the likely tool or technique before testing. I would also organize screenshots and commands as I go so the final write-up is easier to complete.
